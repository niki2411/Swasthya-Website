Yoga Pose: Utthita Parsvakonasana

Utthita Parsvakonasana (Extended Side Angle Pose) is a powerful standing pose that strengthens and stretches the legs, hips, and torso while opening the chest and shoulders.

Instructions:

  • Start in Warrior II pose with your right foot forward.
  • Bend your right knee at a 90-degree angle, ensuring itâ€™s aligned above the ankle.
  • Place your right hand on the floor or a block outside your right foot.
  • Extend your left arm over your left ear, creating a straight line from fingertips to heel.
  • Keep your chest open and gaze up toward your top hand.
  • Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute, then switch sides.

Benefits:

  • Strengthens legs, knees, and ankles.
  • Stretches the groins, spine, waist, chest, and shoulders.
  • Improves stamina and endurance.
  • Stimulates abdominal organs and aids digestion.
