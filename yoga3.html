Yoga Pose: Padmasana (Lotus Pose)

Padmasana, or Lotus Pose, is a classic seated posture used for meditation and pranayama. It promotes physical stability and calms the mind, helping to deepen the practice of meditation.

Instructions:

  • Sit with your legs stretched out in front of you.
  • Bend your right knee and place your right foot on your left thigh, sole facing upward.
  • Now bend your left knee and place your left foot on your right thigh in the same manner.
  • Keep your spine erect and rest your hands on your knees in a mudra of your choice.
  • Close your eyes and focus on your breathing. Hold the pose for a few minutes.

Benefits:

  • Improves posture and strengthens the spine.
  • Calms the brain and helps relieve stress and anxiety.
  • Stimulates the pelvic region and improves digestion.
  • Prepares the body and mind for meditation and pranayama.
