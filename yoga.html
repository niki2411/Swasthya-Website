Yoga Pose:Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

Instructions:

  • Lie on your stomach with a relaxed position. Cross your hands in front and let your head rest upon them.
  • Bring your legs together closely. Let your forehead lie lightly on the ground.
  • Shift your arms so your palms are beside your shoulders, elbows bent and facing upwards.
  • Lift your elbows lightly off the ground. Breathe in and lift the head, pushing the chin forward.
  • Slowly bring your chest off the floor, with your lower abdomen still compressed against the floor.
  • Visualize a snake raising its hood feel the stretch down your spine.
  • Hold the pose, breathing slowly and keeping the lift.
  • Release slowly, bringing your torso down, head still lifted, until your body is completely unrolled and lies flat on the mat.

Benefits:

  • Strengthens the spine, spinal extensors, glutes, and hamstrings.
  • Stretches the front body, including the chest, abdominals, and psoas muscles.
  • Helps correct poor posture by countering the common forward hunch.
  • Opens the chest and lungs, promoting deeper breathing.
  • Enhances spinal flexibility and overall mobility.
