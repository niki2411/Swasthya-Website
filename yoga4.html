Yoga Pose:Trikonasana (Triangle pose)

Trikonasana or Triangle Pose stretches the body, improves flexibility, and stimulates abdominal organs. It also strengthens the legs, knees, and ankles while relieving stress.

Instructions:

  • Stand with feet wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Turn the right foot outward to the right side.
  • Stretch both arms sideways to shoulder level, with both arms in one straight line.
  • Breathe in, then breathe out as you lean to the right, keeping the torso in alignment do not lean forward.
  • Bend your right knee slightly and put your right hand on your right foot or shin.
  • Keep your arms in vertical alignment, and turn your left palm facing forward.
  • Look up at your left hand in the end position.
  • Breathe in and return to the initial position with arms stretched.
  • Repeat on the left side. That is one round.
  • Practice 5 to 10 rounds

Benefits:

  • Strengthens the muscles along the sides of the trunk, waist, and back of the legs.
  • Stimulates the nervous system and helps relieve nervous tension and mild depression.
  • Improves digestion and strengthens the pelvic region.
